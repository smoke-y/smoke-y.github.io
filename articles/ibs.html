IBS.HTML

IBS. A disease crafted by Satan himself. Well, here is how I learn't how to live with it-

Forget your craving

It's not worth it. Your only 2 metrics are a) nutrition and b) is it digestible?

Keep track of general patterns

My IBS flares on 1) Changing kitchen: Whenever I go to home from hostel or go to hostel from home, my ibs flares, which I suppress with eldicet. 2) Consuming non-veg: I switched to veg after I was able to digest protein shake.

Slow introduction to food items that are not home cooked

Never eat an entire packet of Doritos. Eat until your stomach starts getting uneasy. Wait a few days. Repeat. In fact, use this on any food item that you can't digest. The only exceptions would be food items that your stomach just doesn't want you to eat. For me it was non-veg. How does one know? Unfortunately, experience. At the time of writing, I have had IBS for almost an year and now I know my stomach has 2 types of uneasiness- 1)"Hey, could you just stop eating whatever you are eating? Im kinda having a hard time here" and 2)"wtf are you doing you idiot". Non-veg causes type 2 uneasiness and almost all other food falls under type 1. Type 2 uneasiness takes moths for my ibs to stop flaring and type 1 takes any where from few hours to days.

Physical activity- cardio

I lost 10KG under 4 moths. I get sick whenever I ate food that falls under type 2 uneasiness. It didn't matter. I ran 3k almost every day. This helped me a lot. In fact I read a research paper recomending patients with IBS to perform activities that increase heart rate for at least 10min.

Doctors are not that usefull

I got an endoscopy, colonoscopy, blood test, B12 injection, etc... At the end, the doctor just threw his hands up and said "Welp, I don't know". Don't get me wrong. He introduced me to tablets such as eldicet which was very usefull. But at the end of the day, only your observations are helpful. I remember seeing a post on the r/ibs subreddit, that anon got well after they started to consume anti-depressants. What. How. I researched and found out about the gut-brain theory which leads me to the next point-

Gut-brain theory

Gut-brain theory is one of the leading theories trying to explain IBS. In my experience, this theory works pretty well. Get 8 hours of good quality sleep every day. Stay at places that does not put mental stress even if the food you get at this place is not home cooked. I followed the above 2 along with cardio and staying away from food causing type 2 irritation and it did me wonders.

Conclusion

This diseace drained my time/energy/happiness. I spent almost half a semester at my home as a shut in, while my friends back at college where going out and eating whatever they wanted. Whatever. Here are my 2 cents:
1)Your'e gonna have a lot of time alone(at least I did). Use it well.
